Botanical Profile and Native Regions

English Name: Lotus
Scientific Name: Nelumbo nucifera
Class: Magnoliopsida
Lotus flower is India’s national flower and holds sacred significance in Hinduism and Buddhism. Native to South Asia and Australia, it can be propagated from seeds or tubers. Plants grown from seeds will not bloom in first year and typically begin flowering in second year. To improve germination, hard seed coat must be scarified by rubbing it with sandpaper or nicking it with clippers. This allows moisture to penetrate, enabling the seed to sprout successfully. The lotus is celebrated not only for its spiritual symbolism but also for its resilience and beauty in aquatic environments.

Lotus Seed Germination and Planting

After scarifying seed coat, soak seeds in warm water(24–27°C) until they swell. Replace water daily and continue soaking until sprouts emerge, typically within 4–5 days. Once the sprouts reach 6 inches in length, transplant them into prepared containers. "Container Preparation: "" Use a wide-mouthed, watertight plastic pot(11–19 liters capacity). Layer the bottom with 2 parts clay and top with 1 part river sand, creating a total substrate depth of 6 inches. This setup mimics natural aquatic conditions, promoting healthy root development. Maintain consistent moisture and warmth to ensure robust growth.

Lotus Cultivation Techniques

For lotus cultivation, ensure the pot has soil depth of 2–4 inches. Water depth varies by variety: 18 inches for tall-stemmed types and 2–12 inches for dwarf varieties. In colder climates, maintain water temperature using warm water or aquatic heaters. Seed-grown lotus plants rarely bloom in their first year, and fertilization should be minimal during this period. In Myanmar (Yangon), growers cultivate both imported lotus varieties and native types such as white lotus, red lotus, and the sacred Paduma lotus. These plants are propagated from purchased seedlings or seeds.

Benefits of Lotus Seeds

Lotus seeds are mature stamens from the center of lotus flower in a green outer shell with a creamy white kernel inside. To eat them, the slightly bitter green shell is removed, revealing the tender, sweet white core. Rich in anti-aging enzymes, lotus seeds promote radiant youthful skin and improve skin elasticity. Their flavonoid content helps reduce skin inflammation and irritation. They support the health of vital organs like kidneys and spleen, enhancing overall wellness. Whether enjoyed fresh, dried, or in traditional remedies, lotus seeds offer a blend of culinary delight and holistic health benefits rooted in Asian wellness traditions.
