Botanical Profile and Native Regions

Burmese Name - Sampanepan,Sabal (Jasmine Flower)
English Name - Jasmine
Botanical Name - Jasminum sambac
Family - Oleaceae
Sabal is native to Myanmar and Southern Arabia. It thrives in diverse soil types, from sandy to clayey soils, though it grows in fertile, nutrient-rich conditions. This perennial shrub prefers hot and humid climates, well-suited to tropical and subtropical regions. While jasmine is categorized as a shrub, its long-lived nature allows it to persist and bloom for many years.

How to Grow Jasmine Plants

Jasmine is not typically grown from seeds. Instead, it is propagated through methods such as stem layering, division, or cuttings. To propagate by layering, a soft stem is buried in the soil. After about a month, roots will emerge from the nodes. Once rooted, the stem can be cut from the parent plant and replanted. Transplant suckers (shoots growing from the base of the plant) with their roots intact to a new location. For cuttings, select a stem with three to five nodes and trim it. Bury three nodes in the soil and water regularly. Roots will develop from the nodes within about one and a half months, after which the cutting can be planted.

Preparation and Plant Care

In the rainy season, jasmine can be cultivated without raised beds in areas with minimal waterlogging or with raised beds in waterlogged areas. Construct raised beds with dimensions of 30 feet (length) x 5 feet (width) x 1 foot (thickness), with 6 inches above ground and 6 inches below ground. Leave a 1-foot pathway between each bed for accessibility. After planting, press the base of the plant firmly into the soil and water thoroughly. Jasmine flowers bloom from young, tender branches. During early winter (the pruning season), trim the tips of trailing or overgrown branches to stimulate the growth of new shoots and buds.

Medicinal Benefits of Jasmine Flower Herbal Tea

Jasmine herbal tea is known for aiding in weight loss, alleviating liver-related ailments such as hepatitis, jaundice, and abdominal pain caused by liver cirrhosis or diarrhea, and reducing stress. It also lowers the risk of breast cancer and lung cancer. For those managing diabetes, it helps regulate blood sugar levels and supports overall metabolic balance. Jasmine tea promotes regular bowel movements, stimulates the production of digestive enzymes, and improves digestion. To reduce facial wrinkles, it is recommended to drink 2 to 3 cups of jasmine herbal tea daily for consistent benefits.
