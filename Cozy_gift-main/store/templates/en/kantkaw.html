Botanical Profile and Native Regions

English Name: Ceylon Ironwood Tree
Botanical Name: Mesua ferrea
The Ceylon Ironwood Tree, known locally as Kant Kaw, belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family. It is a slow-growing tree renowned for its elegant shape, vibrant green foliage, and large, fragrant white flowers. Thriving in tropical and humid climates, the Kant Kaw is native to regions such as Sri Lanka, India, Nepal, Myanmar, Thailand, the Philippines, Malaysia, and Sumatra.

Soil Requirements for Growing Kant Kaw

Ceylon Ironwood trees (Mesua ferrea) thrive in annual temperatures of 30–35°C and prefer yearly rainfall between 118–157 inches (300–400 cm). They require well-draining, moisture-retentive soil with a slightly acidic pH level of 5–5.5. These conditions are critical for healthy growth, as the species is highly sensitive to waterlogged soil and extreme temperature fluctuations.

Seeds: Germination and Viability

Ceylon Ironwood (Mesua ferrea) seeds are easy to manage and exhibit fast, high-quality germination. The germination rate ranges between 30–70%, with seeds typically sprouting within 11–14 days. Notably, dry, brown seeds can achieve a 100% germination rate. However, seed viability declines sharply after 2–4 months, requiring timely sowing for optimal results.

Cultivation Guidelines and Uses of Ceylon Ironwood

Seedlings propagated from seeds are ready for transplanting when they reach a height of 45–50 cm (17–19 inches). hold significant economic value in traditional medicine and local industries. For small-scale or ornamental cultivation, maintain a spacing of 20–25 feet (6–7.5 meters) between trees to ensure healthy growth.
