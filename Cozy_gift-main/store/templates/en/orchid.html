Essential Facts About Orchidaceae Family

English Name: Orchid
Botanical Name: Orchidaceae
Subspecies Classification: Liliopsida
Orchids are classified into two groups based on their growth patterns: monopodial orchids (vertical growers) and sympodial orchids (horizontal growers). Their growth habits influence cultivation and propagation methods. Orchids are categorized into two types: year-round blooming varieties and seasonal bloomers, which flower only once a year. Orchid Soil Preparation and Planting Techniques

Sympodial orchids, such as Cattleya,Cymbidium, and Dendrobium, are typically grown in clay or plastic pots. Orchids require moderate moisture and dislike waterlogged conditions so their pots must have ample drainage holes. To prevent clogging, place broken bricks or pottery shards at the base of the pot before adding growing media like coconut husk or organic compost. Monopodial orchids, such as Vanda, are mounted on wooden planks or stakes. Species like Spider Orchids, Bone Orchids, and Aranda hybrids(monopodial types) are planted directly in beds. For bed cultivation, dig trenches and layer the bottom with compost, decomposed cow manure, brick fragments, and coconut husk before placing the plants.

Orchid Pest and Disease Prevention

Orchids are commonly affected by pests such as scale insects, thrips, mealybugs, spider mites, aphids, snails, and slugs. To manage these pests, mix a suitable insecticide at a ratio of 1 teaspoon per gallon of water and apply it to the plants. Orchids are also vulnerable to fungal, viral, and bacterial infections at any time. Infected plant parts should be promptly pruned and discarded. For prevention, spray a fungicide weekly.

Orchids and Their Health Benefits

Orchids reduce stress and promote relaxation. Rich in calcium and magnesium, they support bone and dental health, helping to prevent conditions like osteoporosis and strengthen teeth. The antioxidants present in orchids protect against skin damage and chronic diseases, including cancer while aiding in cellular repair. Their dietary fiber and hydration properties promote a healthy digestive system, preventing issues like diarrhea and constipation. Orchids contain vitamins C and E, which enhance eye health, and vitamin A, combined with antioxidants, to maintain youthful, radiant skin.
